# Unit 6 – Formal Modelling vs Automated Testing Debate

## Seminar Topic
Formal Modelling vs Automated Testing  
"Formal modelling is the best way of ensuring a system is secure by design."

---

## Description of Activity

In Unit 6, the seminar focused on a structured debate about whether formal modelling is the best approach to ensure that a system is secure by design. Each team was assigned a position either supporting or opposing the statement. Our team prepared a short presentation and discussed the strengths and weaknesses of formal modelling compared with automated testing.

The aim of this activity was to understand how different development methodologies affect system security, reliability, and correctness. The debate also required us to analyse real-world development processes and evaluate which techniques are most effective in secure systems architecture.

---

## What I Learned

From this seminar, I learned that formal modelling is important during the early stages of system design because it allows developers to identify vulnerabilities before implementation begins. By using models, diagrams, and structured analysis, it is possible to reduce the risk of security flaws being introduced into the system.

However, I also learned that automated testing is necessary to verify that the system works correctly in real conditions. In distributed systems, problems such as latency, packet loss, and unexpected behaviour cannot always be predicted during the modelling stage. Testing allows these problems to be detected and fixed during development.

During our team project, we experienced this directly. In the design stage, we created a model of the system and identified possible vulnerabilities. Later, when implementing the Python prototype, we discovered additional problems that only appeared during testing, such as replay attacks, duplicate messages, and excessive retries. This showed that both modelling and testing are required to build a secure system.

## Reflection

This seminar helped me understand that secure system design cannot rely on only one method. Formal modelling helps prevent errors, but testing is required to confirm that the system works in practice. In our project, using both approaches allowed us to improve the reliability and security of the final implementation.

If I were to repeat this project, I would spend more time improving the design model before coding, and also perform more automated testing earlier in the development process.

Overall, Unit 6 helped me understand the importance of combining design, testing, and teamwork when developing secure distributed systems.
