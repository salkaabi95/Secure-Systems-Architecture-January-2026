Unit 6
Formal Modelling vs Automated Testing Debate
In Unit 6, the seminar focused on a debate discussing whether formal modelling is the best way to ensure that a system is secure by design. Each team was assigned a position either supporting or opposing the statement, and we were required to prepare a short presentation to defend our argument. This activity encouraged critical thinking about different approaches to secure system development and helped demonstrate how design methods influence system reliability and security.
During the preparation for the debate, our team analysed the advantages of formal modelling compared to automated testing. Formal modelling allows designers to identify security flaws at an early stage of the development life cycle, before implementation begins. This supports the principle of secure-by-design, where vulnerabilities are reduced before code is written. However, we also discussed that automated testing is necessary to verify real system behaviour, especially in distributed environments where unpredictable conditions such as latency, packet loss, and concurrency issues may occur.
This discussion was directly related to the work completed in the team project. In our project, we first created a design model and identified vulnerabilities using structured analysis. Later, during implementation, we discovered additional issues that were only visible during testing, such as replay acceptance and excessive retries. This showed that both formal modelling and testing are necessary to produce a secure and reliable system.
The seminar helped me understand that secure systems architecture requires a combination of theoretical modelling and practical validation. Formal models provide structure, while testing confirms real behaviour. This learning outcome links to the module requirement to critically analyse solutions and apply appropriate methodologies to develop secure distributed systems.
From this activity, I developed several professional skills, including teamwork, communication, and critical analysis. Preparing the presentation required collaboration and clear explanation of technical ideas. The debate also improved my confidence in discussing technical decisions and defending design choices.
Overall, Unit 6 helped me understand the importance of combining modelling, testing, and teamwork when developing secure systems in a professional environment.
